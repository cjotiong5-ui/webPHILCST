Mission & Vision

The guiding principles that shape everything we do at PhilCST.

Mission

PhilCST provides quality education to students who are imbued with strong moral character through a well-balanced research and community oriented learning environment that develops critical thinking for maximum development of an individual's talents and capabilities.

Vision

PhilCST envisions to produce graduates fully equipped with knowledge, values, and skills, and who are globally competitive in their profession ever ready to render quality services to society and the nation.

1994
Brief History

A Tradition of Excellence Continues

On July 16, 1990, a powerful earthquake devastated Dagupan City, damaging leading educational institutions. These circumstances led Mrs. Lourdes S. Fernandez — inspired by the national vision of "Philippines 2000" — to establish a school outside the city to serve students and professionals.

In 1993, the Founder built a four-storey building in Nalsian, Calasiao, Pangasinan — the Philippine College of Science and Technology (PhilCST) — the first college institution in the municipality, just 5 kilometers from Dagupan City.

On June 4, 1995, PhilCST formally began operations, offering degree and non-degree courses anchored on recognizing every human being as a potential agent of positive change.
